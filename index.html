Dorothy Dandridge

One of the most famous African-American actresses to have a successful Hollywood career


Image from ThoughtCo

Dorothy Jean Dandridge was a singer, dancer, and film and theatre actress.

You can view more about her on IMDb.


Here is a snapshot timeline of Dorothy Dandridge's life:

1922

Born in Cleveland, Ohio to Ruby Dandridge and Cyril Dandridge

Early 1930's

Spent three years touring churches in the South with older sister Vivian. The duo was known as The Wonder Children

1934

Mother moved the family to Hollywood once the Great Depression was in full swing as bookings dried up for the girls. The girls were enrolled in dancing school met Etta Jones, who joined the group which was renamed "The Dandridge Sisters"

1935

The Dandridge Sisters got their big break in the musical The Big Broadcast of 1936

1937

The Dandridge Sisters had a small part in the Marx Brother's film A Day at the Races

1938

The trio appeared in the film Going Places and sang with Louis Armstrong.

Also in 1938 the trio was booked in the famed Cotton Club in New York. The girls and their caretaker moved to New York and on the first day of rehearsals for the show Dorothy met Harold Nicholas, who she began dating and later married

1940

Landed a small part in the film Four Shall Die

1941

Landed a small part in the film Lady From Louisiana and Sundown. She also sang and danced with the Nicholas Brothers in the film Sun Valley Serenade along with the Glenn Miller Band

1942

On September 6, 1942 she married Harold Nicholas

1943

On September 2, 1943, gave birth to her daughter Harolyn (Lynn) Suzanne Dandridge

1950

Played a jungle queen in Tarzan's Peril

1951

Divorced Harold Nicholas

1952

Played the lead in MGM's Bright Road

1954

Played the lead role in Carmen Jones. Her role in Carmen Jones also earned her the privilege of being the first black woman to grace the cover of Life magazine

1955

At the Academy Awards she was nominated for the Best Actress in a Leading role award for her role in Carmen Jones. She was the first African-American to be nominated in that category

1957

Cast in the film Island in the Sun

1958

Secured a movie deal in Europe for the French/Italian film Tamango. She was also cast in the film The Decks Ran Red

1959

Offered the lead in the film Porgy and Bess. Also married Jack Denison on June 22

1962

Amid financial woes and abuse allegations she divorced Jack Denison in November. She also filed for bankruptcy this year and lost everything including her Hollywood home and cars

1965

In the midst of a comeback to resurrect her career, Dorothy Dandridge was found dead on September 8th in a Hollywood apartment from an overdose of prescription drugs
